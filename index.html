Frequently asked questions

What do we do?

Immersive Sensory Art; we transform visits into unique experiences. We are digital artists; with this technology, we sharpen the 5 senses of our clients. With our own developed techniques, we give users the ability to navigate through common areas, choose their desired floor, and navigate within the chosen units. We take our clients to the future—allowing them to even sit and enjoy the view from their future apartment, all in real time and with the highest graphic quality ever seen.

What is the investment price, and how long does it take to complete?

Channeling our inner Einstein, we can confidently say that the answer to this question is ‘relative,’ much like his famous theory of relativity. It varies based on the project’s conditions, such as square footage, dimensions, number of typologies, the client’s needs, and the peculiarities of each project. Contact us today, and we’ll provide a tailored response specific to your project’s needs.

Why don't we use the VR goggles?

We follow the teachings of Steve Jobs, whose concepts, despite being old, still apply today. According to Jobs, the usability of technology should be an extension of our bodies. He demonstrated this by eliminating the need for a stylus for cellphones and introducing touch with our own fingers. Following this masterclass and having studied the use of VR in the real estate market, we realized two factors that make us focus on technologies that do not require goggles:

1- There is a significant risk that the 82.7% of the US population who have not used VR technologies could experience severe vertigo and nausea upon exposure. Certainly, these are not sensations you want your clients to associate with your brand or project when leaving a sales gallery.

2- VR creates a very individualistic experience; when customers are purchasing their future home, it is rare that they go alone, often bringing spouses, children, and friends. With this in mind, the loss of time and engagement between the broker and the clients turns out to be unfeasible. And, as we know, Time is Money!
